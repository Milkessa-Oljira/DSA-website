The Modularity Paradox

Exploring why human intelligence emerges from non-modular computation
while AI remains trapped in modular thinking

üß† The Core Hypothesis

Human intelligence operates on a fascinating paradox: our underlying neural computation is completely non-modular, yet we interpret and express our thoughts in modular ways (language, concepts, plans).

Meanwhile, current AI systems are built on modular computation (linear algebra, discrete layers) and produce modular outputs. This fundamental mismatch may be why we don't have AGI yet.

üö´ Why Modularity Breaks Down in Physical Reality

‚ùå No True Isolation

Every system is embedded in its environment. Quantum entanglement shows particles can be instantly correlated across vast distances, violating modular independence.

‚ùå Interconnected Emergence

Complex phenomena like consciousness and weather emerge from interdependence. You can't study one module and predict everything.

‚ùå Observer Entanglement

In quantum mechanics, the observer cannot be cleanly separated from the observed. Measurement fundamentally disturbs the system.

Modularity is an epistemic convenience, not an ontological truth.
